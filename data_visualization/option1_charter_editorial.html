About

What I do

I'm a Master's candidate in Computational Analysis and Public Policy at the University of Chicago, where I work at the intersection of data science, economics, and public policy. I build models and pipelines that turn raw, messy data — satellite imagery, century-old archives, property tax records — into evidence people can actually use to make decisions.

My work spans applied machine learning, causal inference, and geospatial analysis. I've applied these methods to housing markets, transit infrastructure, water systems, and policing across Brazil, Senegal, India, and the United States — and I care as much about explaining the result clearly as about getting it right.

I'm also a committed cyclist — which is part of why the way cities move people, and who they're built for, is the question I keep coming back to.
